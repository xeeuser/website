Common Questions

What Clients Ask Before Starting

How long does a typical project take? +
It depends entirely on scope. Our shortest engagements run 8–10 weeks. Complex SaaS platforms typically run 16–24 weeks. We give a precise timeline after the Discovery phase — never before, because before Discovery we would be guessing.
Do you work with early-stage startups? +
Yes — if you have a validated problem, a defined scope, and a realistic budget. We have worked with pre-seed founders and Series B companies. What matters is not your funding stage — it is the seriousness of your intent.
What happens after launch? +
Every project includes a 30-day post-launch support window at no extra cost. After that, we offer retainer arrangements for ongoing development, feature additions, or infrastructure management.
How do we communicate during the project? +
You get a dedicated Slack channel on day one. We send a sprint update every Friday. Loom videos for complex demos. Jira for task visibility. You are never left guessing what we are working on.
Can we start without a full spec? +
Absolutely. Most clients come to us with a problem, not a spec. That is what Discovery is for. We help you arrive at a precise scope together — and we will tell you honestly if what you have described does not require a custom build.

Who We Work With

We Are Not for Everyone.
That Is the Point.

We made a deliberate choice to stay small, take fewer clients, and do exceptional work for the ones we take. That means we are very specific about who we work with.

The Right Fit

Who We're Built For

We work best with founders and product leaders who have a clear problem, respect engineering timelines, and are building something that genuinely needs to scale. Our best clients treat us as a technical co-founder, not a vendor.

✓
Series A or growth-stage startup / scaling SME. You have validated your product and you are ready to build infrastructure that scales.
✓
You have a defined product roadmap and clear KPIs. We do not help figure out whether to build — we help build what has already been decided.
✓
You value engineering quality over lowest price. You understand that the cheapest option is often the most expensive one in 12 months.
✓
You communicate directly and respect process. You treat your engineering team as a partner, not a service desk.
The Wrong Fit

Who This Is Not For

We are not saying these are bad clients. We are saying they would not get the most from working with us — and we would not do our best work for them either. Better to be honest upfront.

✗
"$500 WordPress or landing page" requests. We are not the right fit for this, and there are great agencies that are.
✗
Agencies looking to white-label our work. Our clients know they are working with Xuneel. That is non-negotiable.
✗
Founders with no roadmap seeking a "quick build." We do Discovery first. Always. There is no shortcut to building the right thing.
✗
Clients who want to skip Discovery and QA. We do not ship untested software. If you need to skip QA to hit a date, that date is wrong.
✗
Anyone expecting production software in under 3 weeks. Good software takes time. If someone is promising otherwise, run.
